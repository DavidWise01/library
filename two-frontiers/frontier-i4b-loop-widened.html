The Loop, Widened

where the two frontiers meet · pamphlet 8 · expanded

Pamphlet 8 closed the loop. This one widens it. The two frontiers of this set — quantum and intelligence — have stopped running in parallel and started feeding each other. And the oldest question of the whole series, "what is the unit?", is live again: bit, qubit, and now qutrit. Here's the merge as the frame, and the unit ladder as the detail.

First, a myth to clear
  • Quantum advantage is not "ternary" or "3×3×3." Standard quantum computing runs on the qubit — a two-state unit. Its power isn't more values per unit; it's superposition, entanglement, and interference.
  • The scaling that matters is exponential in the number of qubits: n qubits span a space of size 2ⁿ. Stacking states per unit is the wrong picture — it's binary units, combined.
  • "Quantum advantage" means doing what classical machines practically can't — speed and intractability — not a base-3 number system.
  • "Ternary" splits in two: classical ternary (base-3 logic, the 1958 Setun computer, balanced −1/0/+1) is a separate lineage that never beat binary. The qutrit is the quantum three-level unit — and that is the real, current frontier (below).

The geometry leaps

A bit is two points; a qubit is a sphere; a qutrit and beyond live in higher-dimensional spaces.

Devoret's image a ququart ≈ a sphere in seven dimensions

so each rung is a genuinely larger state space, not just "more values."

+1 Michel Devoret, who framed it this way, shared the 2025 Nobel Prize in Physics — this is current, top-of-field work.

06

Quantum flips the classical rule

Classically, adding states per unit doesn't pay — which is why binary won. Quantum mechanics reverses that.

contrast Setun lost; qutrits may win

so "more levels" is a liability classically but an asset quantumly.

+1 most quantum hardware already has extra energy levels — usually ignored; qudits harness them instead of wasting them.

The core of it
  • The frame: the AI and quantum frontiers now feed each other — transformer decoders (AlphaQubit) and RL stabilize quantum hardware, while quantum ML and quantum cross-entropy carry Shannon's quantities into quantum learning.
  • The handshake: quantum cross-entropy's minimum is the von Neumann entropy — pamphlet 5 and pamphlet 2, one equation.
  • The detail: the unit ladder runs bit → qubit → qutrit → qudit — each a larger state space, not merely "more values."
  • The reversal: adding states per unit doesn't pay classically (why binary and Setun's ternary settled out), but can pay quantumly — qudits harness levels most hardware already has.
  • The milestone: Yale (2025) error-corrected a qutrit and ququart beyond break-even with the GKP code, optimized by reinforcement learning — the merge and the ladder in a single result.
Settled vs. frontier — and the myth, once more
  • The correction stands: quantum advantage comes from superposition and entanglement scaling as 2ⁿ, not from a base-3 unit. "Ternary" is either classical (Setun) or the qutrit — don't fuse them with "quantum advantage."
  • Settled: AlphaQubit's results, the Yale qudit error-correction demonstration, and quantum cross-entropy as a defined quantity are real, published (Nature, 2024–25).
  • Frontier: quantum machine learning's practical advantage is not yet proven on real-world problems — promising and hyped in equal measure. Treat bold QML claims with care.
  • Frontier: qudits are early — richer state spaces bring more complex errors; whether they beat plain qubits at scale is genuinely open.
  • This widens — but doesn't replace — pamphlet 8. The honest close of the whole series still holds: a measured bit is not a grasped meaning, and a promising frontier is not a finished one.
